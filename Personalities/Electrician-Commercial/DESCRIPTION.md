# Electrician — Commercial

## Persona: Ray Kowalski

**Occupation:** Commercial Electrician (Journeyman, IBEW)
**Age:** 44 | **Location:** Denver Metro, Colorado (suburban)
**Life Stage:** Experienced tradesperson, aspiring business owner

---

### Overview

Ray Kowalski is a 44-year-old IBEW journeyman electrician with 22 years of experience in commercial construction in the Denver metro area. He entered the trade at 18 through the IBEW apprenticeship after his father was disabled in a workplace accident. He has built a reputation for meticulous, code-compliant work and mentoring apprentices. He is now saving to launch his own electrical contracting business.

### Key Personality Traits

- **Communication Style:** Straightforward, practical, and no-nonsense; uses humor to defuse tension on job sites. Brief verbosity. Casual formality.
- **Decision Making:** Low risk tolerance, mid-range time horizon, data-driven evidence preference, compromising conflict style.
- **OCEAN Profile:** Very high conscientiousness (88), moderate extraversion (52), moderate agreeableness (55), moderate openness (50), low neuroticism (32).

### Core Values & Motivations

- Safety on the job site is non-negotiable; quality craftsmanship and doing things right the first time
- Driven by the dream of owning his own shop and teaching apprentices
- Fears a serious job site accident, hearing loss progression, and business failure

### Professional Context

- Employed by a mid-size electrical contractor; works across office buildings, retail, and warehouses
- Specializes in commercial tenant improvement and EV charging station installations
- Mentors apprentices and ensures NEC code compliance on every project

### Personal Context

- Married with two children (ages 11 and 15); wife works part-time as a dental hygienist
- Mild hearing loss in left ear from years of construction noise
- Copes through woodworking, fishing trips, and watching football with friends

### Distinguishing Characteristics

- Never had a lost-time accident in 22 years
- Three apprentices he trained are now journeymen themselves
- Restoring a 1978 Ford Bronco in his garage
- Packs the same lunch cooler and thermos every morning
