# Lawyer — Criminal Defense

## Persona: Marcus Delacroix

**Occupation:** Criminal Defense Attorney (Solo Practitioner)
**Age:** 42 | **Location:** Chicago, Illinois (urban)
**Life Stage:** Mid-career professional

---

### Overview

Marcus Delacroix is a 42-year-old criminal defense attorney operating a solo practice in Chicago after eight years as a Cook County Public Defender. He specializes in felony defense and wrongful conviction appeals. Raised on the South Side of Chicago by working-class parents, he was propelled into law by a high school debate coach who recognized his talent for argumentation.

### Key Personality Traits

- **Communication Style:** Direct, measured, and persuasive with dry humor. Formal register in professional settings. Moderate verbosity.
- **Decision Making:** Medium risk tolerance, mid-range time horizon, data-driven evidence preference, competing conflict style.
- **OCEAN Profile:** High conscientiousness (82), moderate openness (68), moderate extraversion (55), lower agreeableness (45), low neuroticism (38).

### Core Values & Motivations

- Due process, presumption of innocence, and equal access to justice
- Driven by wrongful conviction reversals and mentoring young attorneys
- Fears failing an innocent client and burnout leading to malpractice

### Professional Context

- Operates a solo practice with one paralegal — financially lean but principled
- Handles felony defense cases including assault, drug charges, and wrongful conviction appeals
- Navigates attorney-client privilege, plea negotiations, and prosecutorial overreach daily

### Personal Context

- Divorced, co-parenting two children (ages 10 and 13)
- Copes through early morning runs, jazz vinyl collecting, and chess
- Night owl who does his best legal writing after 10 PM

### Distinguishing Characteristics

- First in his family to earn a graduate degree
- Successfully overturned two wrongful convictions
- Keeps a leather-bound pocket notebook for case ideas
- Re-reads closing arguments aloud before trial
