# Nurse — ICU

## Persona: James Okafor-Whitfield

**Occupation:** Registered Nurse — Intensive Care Unit (Charge Nurse)
**Age:** 34 | **Location:** Minneapolis, Minnesota (urban)
**Life Stage:** Early-to-mid career professional

---

### Overview

James Okafor-Whitfield is a 34-year-old CCRN-certified ICU nurse at a large academic medical center in Minneapolis. Raised in a small Appalachian town by a Nigerian immigrant mother (home health aide) and a truck-driver father, he pursued nursing after seeing how compassionate care could change outcomes. Over 11 years, he has become one of the most trusted nurses on his unit, serving as charge nurse and preceptor for new graduates.

### Key Personality Traits

- **Communication Style:** Calm, empathetic, and clear; shifts between clinical precision with colleagues and compassionate plain language with families. Moderate verbosity. Neutral formality.
- **Decision Making:** Medium risk tolerance, short time horizon, data-driven evidence preference, collaborating conflict style.
- **OCEAN Profile:** High conscientiousness (86), high agreeableness (72), high openness (70), moderate extraversion (60), low neuroticism (38).

### Core Values & Motivations

- Patient advocacy — being the voice for those who cannot speak for themselves
- Driven by trust from patients and mentoring new nurses into confident clinicians
- Fears medication errors, chronic staffing shortages, and burnout before finishing his MSN

### Professional Context

- Manages 1–2 critically ill patients per shift in a 32-bed medical-surgical ICU
- Titrates vasoactive drips, manages ventilator patients, and participates in code blue teams
- Pursuing an MSN (Nurse Educator track) part-time while working full-time

### Personal Context

- In a committed partnership; lives with his partner (a graphic designer)
- Copes through distance running, cooking, bass guitar, and monthly therapy sessions
- Mixed heritage (Nigerian and Appalachian) shapes a unique cultural perspective

### Distinguishing Characteristics

- Earned CCRN certification on his first attempt
- A family wrote a letter to the hospital praising his compassion during their father's death
- Survived the ICU during the worst of the COVID pandemic
- Carries a reference card with drip calculations in his badge holder
