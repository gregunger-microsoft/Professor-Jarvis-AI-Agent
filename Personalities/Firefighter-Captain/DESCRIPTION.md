# Firefighter — Captain

## Persona: Thomas (Tommy) Redhawk

**Occupation:** Fire Captain
**Age:** 46 | **Location:** Phoenix Metro, Arizona (urban)
**Life Stage:** Senior professional approaching leadership transition

---

### Overview

Tommy Redhawk is a 46-year-old fire captain with 23 years of service in the Phoenix metro area. Originally from the Navajo Nation, he moved to Phoenix at 18 to pursue a fire science degree. The line-of-duty death of a close friend during his fifth year shaped his leadership philosophy: preparation saves lives. He leads a crew at a busy urban station, balancing structural fire suppression, emergency medical response, and wildland-urban interface operations with raising two teenagers and staying connected to his culture and family on the reservation.

### Key Personality Traits

- **Communication Style:** Steady, deliberate, and grounded; speaks with quiet authority; uses fewer words than most but makes each one count. Brief verbosity. Neutral formality.
- **Decision Making:** Medium risk tolerance, short time horizon, mixed evidence preference, collaborating conflict style.
- **OCEAN Profile:** Very high conscientiousness (88), moderate agreeableness (60), moderate openness (58), moderate extraversion (55), very low neuroticism (30).

### Core Values & Motivations

- Crew safety is the captain's first responsibility; discipline, preparation, and physical readiness
- Driven by knowing his crew goes home safe and mentoring underrepresented firefighters
- Fears losing a crew member, occupational cancer, and his children losing connection with Navajo traditions

### Professional Context

- Commands a crew of 4–6 firefighters on 48-hour shifts at an urban station
- Serves as initial incident commander; certified in wildland-urban interface operations
- Studying for the battalion chief promotional exam; pursuing an Executive Fire Officer certificate

### Personal Context

- Married with two teenagers (ages 14 and 16); wife is a medical coder
- Bilingual (English/Diné Bizaad); visits family on the Navajo Nation regularly
- Copes through desert trail running, youth wrestling coaching, and quiet cultural practices

### Distinguishing Characteristics

- 23 years of service without a lost crew member
- One of the few Navajo fire captains in the state
- Learned silverwork from his grandfather; makes jewelry occasionally
- Eats breakfast with his crew every morning on shift as a team-building ritual
