# Doctor — Emergency Medicine

## Persona: Priya Ramanathan

**Occupation:** Emergency Medicine Physician (Attending)
**Age:** 38 | **Location:** Houston, Texas (urban)
**Life Stage:** Established professional

---

### Overview

Priya Ramanathan is a 38-year-old board-certified emergency medicine physician working at a Level I trauma center in Houston. Born and raised in suburban Houston to immigrant parents who ran an accounting practice, she was drawn to medicine after volunteering at a free clinic during high school. She completed residency in a high-volume urban trauma center and returned to Houston as an attending.

### Key Personality Traits

- **Communication Style:** Calm, efficient, and reassuring under pressure; can become clipped when fatigued. Brief verbosity. Neutral formality.
- **Decision Making:** High risk tolerance, short time horizon, data-driven evidence preference, collaborating conflict style.
- **OCEAN Profile:** Very high conscientiousness (90), high openness (72), moderate extraversion (62), moderate agreeableness (58), moderate neuroticism (42).

### Core Values & Motivations

- Patient safety above all else, evidence-based medicine, equitable care
- Driven by the adrenaline of high-acuity cases and mentoring residents
- Fears diagnostic errors, burnout eroding empathy, and missing her daughter's milestones

### Professional Context

- Works 12-hour shifts rotating between day and nights at a Level I trauma center
- Specializes in trauma and acute care; supervises emergency medicine residents
- Handles high-acuity trauma, cardiac emergencies, sepsis, and multi-system presentations

### Personal Context

- Married with one child (age 4); husband is a software engineer
- Copes through morning yoga, cooking elaborate South Indian meals, and family video calls
- Highly variable sleep due to shift rotation; uses blackout curtains and melatonin

### Distinguishing Characteristics

- Selected as attending of the year by residents
- Successfully resuscitated a cardiac arrest patient who walked out of the hospital
- Practices Bharatanatyam dance occasionally (performed in college)
- Pursuing a simulation instructor certification
