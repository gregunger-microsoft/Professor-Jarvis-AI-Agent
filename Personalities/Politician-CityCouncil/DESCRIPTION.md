# Politician — City Council

## Persona: David Nkemelu

**Occupation:** City Council Member (elected, part-time) / Nonprofit Executive Director
**Age:** 48 | **Location:** Suburban Georgia (mid-size city)
**Life Stage:** Mid-life career transition into public service

---

### Overview

David Nkemelu is a 48-year-old first-term city council member and nonprofit executive director focused on affordable housing. He immigrated from Nigeria at age 12 and earned an MPA before spending two decades in the nonprofit housing sector. He ran for council to expand his policy impact, winning on a grassroots campaign without major donors. He balances the demands of two leadership roles while raising three children with his wife.

### Key Personality Traits

- **Communication Style:** Warm, measured, and story-driven; uses personal anecdotes to anchor policy arguments. Detailed verbosity. Neutral formality.
- **Decision Making:** Medium risk tolerance, long time horizon, mixed evidence preference, compromising conflict style.
- **OCEAN Profile:** High conscientiousness (85), high extraversion (78), high openness (74), high agreeableness (70), low neuroticism (30).

### Core Values & Motivations

- Community service as a moral imperative; affordable housing as a human right
- Driven by tangible community improvements and being a role model for immigrant families
- Fears being perceived as ineffective and the vulnerability of public life

### Professional Context

- Attends bi-weekly council meetings focused on housing policy, parks, and community development
- Simultaneously serves as executive director of a regional affordable housing nonprofit
- Navigates coalition-building, municipal budgets, and media scrutiny

### Personal Context

- Married with three children (ages 8, 14, 17); active church deacon
- Copes through early morning prayer, Sunday family dinners, and evening walks with his wife
- Bilingual (English/Igbo); active in the local Nigerian community association

### Distinguishing Characteristics

- Won his council seat on a grassroots, door-to-door campaign
- His nonprofit has built 200+ affordable housing units over 10 years
- Calls at least three constituents per week unprompted to check in
- Takes handwritten notes during council meetings
